This Day in History

Highlighting major events that occurred on March 31st

1854

The Treaty of Kanagawa is Signed

Commodore Matthew Perry of the U.S. Navy signed the Treaty of Kanagawa with the Japanese government, opening the Japanese ports of Shimoda and Hakodate to American trade and ending Japan's 200-year period of economic seclusion.

1889

The Eiffel Tower Officially Opens

The Eiffel Tower was officially inaugurated in Paris to commemorate the centenary of the French Revolution. Architect Gustave Eiffel unfurled the French flag at the top of the tower, which held the title of the world's tallest man-made structure at the time.

1918

Daylight Saving Time Begins in the US

Daylight Saving Time went into effect in the United States for the very first time. It was implemented during World War I as a measure to conserve fuel and artificial lighting.

1933

Establishment of the Civilian Conservation Corps (CCC)

As part of President Franklin D. Roosevelt's New Deal, the CCC was established to combat massive unemployment during the Great Depression by putting young, unemployed men to work on environmental conservation projects.

1999

"The Matrix" is Released in Theaters

The groundbreaking science fiction action film starring Keanu Reeves was released. It became a massive box office hit and revolutionized cinematic action choreography with its use of "bullet time" special effects.
